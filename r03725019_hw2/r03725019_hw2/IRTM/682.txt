russian president vladimir putin says he will pardon and release american 
businessman edmond pope. a russian court convicted pope wednesday 
on espionage charges and sentenced him to 20 years in prison. pope 
denied the charges and appealed to putin for clemency saying he needs 
urgent medical aid. he has suffered from a rare type of bone cancer. 
the pennsylvania man has been jailed in russia since april on charges 
he tried to obtain secret plans for a high-speed torpedo. pope, who 
runs a technology information company, insisted the plans were freely 
available. the clinton administration is praising the decision to 
release pope.
