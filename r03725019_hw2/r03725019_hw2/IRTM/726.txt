President Clinton has pardoned a former leading member of the House 
of Representatives convicted of corruption charges in 1996. Former 
Chairman of the House Ways and Means committee Dan Rostenkowski was 
among 59 convicted criminals pardoned by the President. He was sentenced 
to 17 months in prison and fined 100,000 dollars after pleading guilty 
to mail fraud. Clinton is also considering clemency for several other 
convicted criminals. BBC News for The World. In London, I am James 
Cowling.
